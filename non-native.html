Compounding the extinction plight of the Hawaiian land snail fauna, Hawaii has more established non-indigenous land snails than any other island or archipelago in the Pacific. Many of these are invasive in natural ecosystems and/or vectors of human and livestock diseases, such as the Rat Lungworm (Angiostrongylus cantonensis). As of 2018, there are now 53 established non-indigenous land snails established in Hawaii.

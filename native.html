WHY ARE NATIVE SPECIES IN DANGER?


LOSS OF DIVERSITY

The Hawaiian land snail fauna is arguably the most diverse in the world in relation to land area: there are over 750 recognized species in only 10 families. Unfortunately, Pacific island land snails hold the highest number of documented extinctions of any taxonomic group since the 1500s. Extinction estimates of Hawaiian land snail families range from 50-95%.


LACK OF CONSERVATION EFFORTS

Some people may believe that land snails and other invertebrates are abundant and thriving, but that is not the case. In reality, invertebrates are underrepresented in conservation management and policies because people incorrectly assume that they have high diversity. We need to recognize this issue and appreciate land snails and other invertebrates that are necessary to our ecosystems!
